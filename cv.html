Experience University of Memphis (Graduate Research Assistant, Aug 2018 - Present)
  • Leading the RELIEF Study, integrating wearable AI and LLMs for personalized stress interventions.
  • Led the nationwide MOODS Study with 122 participants over 100 days, achieving a 10% reduction in self-reported stress and 14 behavior modifications (CHI 2024).
  • Developed a Personal Informatics System with 16 visualizations to promote self-reflection and user engagement.
  • Developed the AI-Triggered Prompting (ATP) framework to optimize prompt timing and predict personalized stressor listings, minimizing interruptions and response burden.
  • Co-author of Pulse-PPG, the first open-source PPG foundation model trained on field data, demonstrating superior generalization across clinical and mobile health applications (Paper).
  • Conducted a duoethnographic study on wearable-integrated LLM chatbots for stress management, showing that brief, tailored interventions are more effective than generic ones.
  • Designed and implemented a dashboard utilizing LLM-assisted evaluations to rank models (GPT-4o, Claude-Sonnet, LLaMA3-8B) based on their ability in generating stress management interventions (GitHub).
  • Developed a framework for evaluating Named Entity Recognition (NER) tasks in Nepali sentences using LLMs (GitHub).
  • Collaborated with researchers at Johns Hopkins University on a field study analyzing the influence of affect and sleep on pain management in sickle cell patients (Journal of Pain 2024).
